What's wrong exactly?

Wrong: tacklewarehouse.com/product.html?=BFOR (no parameter name)
Correct: tacklewarehouse.com/product.html?from=BFOR (has "from" key)

Both appear on the same pages — meaning someone got it right sometimes and wrong other times. Some links also have double errors like ?=BFOR?=BFOR or ?color=RCB?=BFOR (double question mark).

How 10,000 Lake Pages Avoid Thin Content

The question Landon will ask: "Won't Google see template pages as thin content?"

It would be — if we just slapped a weather widget and a Google Places search on 10,000 pages. Here's how we avoid that.

What Makes It Not ThinWhy Google Cares
BFR rating changes daily per lakeProprietary data nobody else has. Fresh every day. Google re-crawls pages with changing data.
Bait Advisor recommendations shift with conditionsAlso proprietary. Different output per lake per day. Can't be scraped from anywhere else.
Dynamic meta descriptions"Lake Fork BFR: 8.4 (Epic) today. Water level 400.28 ft." Changes daily — same technique LakeMonster uses on 17,747 pages.
Tournament history from Bassmaster APIUnique structured data per lake. Multi-year results with weights, anglers, placements.
User-generated contentLunker Club catches tagged to lakes. Guide reviews. Fishing reports. Content Google can't find elsewhere.
Directory listings are self-updatingGuides, marinas, shops update their own profiles. Continuous fresh content without editorial effort.
Editorial content per lakeMine 300+ existing articles for lake-specific content. Seasonal patterns, structure, local knowledge.

The launch strategy that prevents thin pages

RuleWhy
Start with 100 lakes, not 10,000Launch only lakes with rich data — tournament history, multiple guides, active catches. Prove they rank, then scale.
Every page needs at least one editorial sectionLake-specific seasonal patterns, structure tips, local knowledge. AI-assisted but specific to that lake — not generic filler.
Don't build empty pagesA lake with no tournaments, no guides, no catches, and no editorial = thin. Don't publish it until there's enough data.
Internal linking from existing articlesEvery article mentioning Lake Fork links to the Lake Fork page. Builds topical authority, not just page count.
Directories are the long-term moatOnce guides and shops are listing and updating themselves, that's a platform — not a template.
