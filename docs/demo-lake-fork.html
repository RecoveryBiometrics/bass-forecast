Lake Fork Bass Fishing Guide

Lake Fork Reservoir is widely considered the best trophy largemouth bass lake in the United States. Located in the piney woods of East Texas about 80 miles east of Dallas, the 27,264-acre reservoir has produced more ShareLunker entries (13+ lb bass) than any other lake in Texas. The state record largemouth — 18.18 lbs — was caught here in 1992.

Seasonal Patterns

Spring (Mar-May)

Pre-spawn and spawn. Bass move to shallow flats and creek arms. 2-6 ft depth. Target submerged timber and grass lines. Best season for trophy fish. Water temp 58-72°F.

Summer (Jun-Aug)

Bass relate to deep timber and creek channel ledges. 12-25 ft. Early morning topwater bite, then transition to deep cranks and Carolina rigs. Water temp 78-88°F. Fish early or late.

Fall (Sep-Nov)

Shad migration pulls bass into creek arms. Follow the bait. Spinnerbaits, crankbaits, and swimbaits over submerged timber. Schooling activity on the main lake. Water temp 60-78°F.

Winter (Dec-Feb)

Slow but big fish opportunities. Jerkbaits and blade baits on main lake points. 15-30 ft. Patience pays — fewer bites but quality fish. Water temp 42-55°F. Best mid-day when water warms.

Key Structure

Lake Fork is defined by standing timber — thousands of acres of flooded forest that create ambush points at every depth. The key areas are: Creek arms (Little Caney, Big Caney, Birch Creek, Running Creek) which concentrate bait and bass seasonally. Main lake points where creek channels swing close to the bank. Grass lines — hydrilla and milfoil beds that hold fish year-round. The 154 bridge — a classic structure spot with deep water access.

Regulations

Slot limit: All bass 16-24 inches must be released. Only 1 bass over 24" may be kept per day. 5 bass daily bag limit. This slot limit is what made Lake Fork a trophy fishery — it protects the breeding-size fish that produce the next generation of lunkers.

Lake Fork Fishing Reports

Mike R. — Guide Report April 4, 2026

Solid day on the water. Pre-spawn fish stacking up in Big Caney Creek arm in 4-8 ft around submerged timber. Texas-rigged Zoom Trick Worm (watermelon red) was the deal. Caught 14 keepers by noon, best fish 6.8 lbs. Water stained from recent rain — darker colors outperformed. Topwater bite was on fire from 6:30-8 AM before the wind picked up.

14 keepers Best: 6.8 lbs BFR: 7.9
Tommy L. — Weekend Trip March 30, 2026

First time on Lake Fork. Launched at SH 154 ramp (free, easy access). Struggled in the morning but switched to a black/blue jig around standing timber and it clicked. Caught 8 bass, biggest was 4.2 lbs. Water was clear on the main lake, stained in the creeks. Wish I'd brought a frog — saw fish busting on the surface in the grass but couldn't reach them with what I had.

8 bass Best: 4.2 lbs BFR: 6.1
